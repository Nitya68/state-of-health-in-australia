State of Health in Australia

Australia boasts a world-class healthcare system,
recognized as the top-performing country in 2024
by the Commonwealth Fund. The nation’s strong
investment in health services ensures high-quality
care and excellent health outcomes.
As shown on the map, Australia consistently ranks
among countries with high health expenditure per capita,
reflecting its commitment to maintaining and improving
public health.

Chronic conditions in Australia

Chronic conditions by State

 
  

This section will discuss findings from the National Health Survey (NHS).
There is a clear upward trend in the prevalence of chronic
conditions in Australia, particularly in the "1 chronic
condition" and "2 or more chronic conditions" categories.
Conversely, the proportion of individuals with "No selected
chronic condition" has steadily declined, indicating fewer
Australians are free from chronic health issues.

This trend suggests a growing burden of chronic conditions,
likely associated with an aging population or lifestyle
changes. The percentage of people experiencing 1 or more
chronic conditions has risen compared to previous
surveys. In 2022, around half the population in
most states had no chronic conditions, with
Tasmania, South Australia, and Queensland showing
the highest rates of multiple conditions.

Long Term Chronic Conditions across States

The total number of people experiencing selected long-term
health conditions—including arthritis, asthma, back problems,
cancer, COPD, diabetes mellitus, hay fever, heart
disease, hypertension, kidney disease, mental conditions,
and osteoporosis—has increased across all states. Hay
fever has also seen an uptick in all regions.
Notably, comparing the 2022 survey results to those

from 2017-18 reveals a significant rise in the
number of individuals suffering from mental and
behavioral conditions. This trend indicates a growing
awareness, increased reporting, or a potential real
increase in mental health issues across many states.

Proportion of people by Health Risks across States

The heat map shows a consistent pattern in
the distribution of health risks and behaviors
across all Australian states. There are no
stark differences in the proportions, indicating
that these health-related trends are fairly
uniform nationwide. One key observation is that
physical activity guidelines are not being met
by a significant portion of the population in
all states, with medium to darker shades
indicating low adherence. Alcohol consumption,
particularly exceeding the recommended guidelines
and heavy drinking, shows a moderate but
consistent presence across states.

In terms of BMI, overweight and obesity
rates are relatively high, as indicated by
darker shades, reflecting a widespread issue
across the country. Additionally, the low
fruit and vegetable consumption is evident
with consistently darker shades, showing that
many people in each state are not meeting
daily nutritional recommendations. The category
for psychological distress shows moderate levels,
with only slight variations between states.
Interestingly, the smoker status shows a darker
shade for "never smoked", indicating a higher
percentage of people in most states who have
never smoked.
